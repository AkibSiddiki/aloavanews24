Is Bangladesh ready for post-LIBOR era?

The LIBOR (London Inter-Bank Offered Rate) methodology is designed to produce an average rate that is representative of the rates at which large, leading internationally active banks with access to the wholesale, unsecured funding market could fund themselves in such market in particular currencies for certain tenors. LIBOR is currently calculated for five currencies (USD, GBP, EUR, CHF, and JPY) and for seven tenors in respect of each currency (Overnight/Spot Next, One Week, One Month, Two Months, Three Months, Six Months and 12 Months). This results in the publication of 35 individual rates (one for each currency and tenor combination) every applicable London business day.
